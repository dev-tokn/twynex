# Twynex Competitive Landscape Notes

This document captures how adjacent companies describe the cross-border payments problem and what Twynex can learn from their positioning.

## Adjacent Companies

### Wise / Wise Platform

Positioning:

- Fast, transparent, lower-cost international payments.
- APIs for banks, financial institutions, and businesses to embed cross-border payments.
- Multi-currency accounts, global payments, receiving money, and card issuing.

Useful references:

- https://wise.com/platform
- https://wise.com/help/articles/2958106/whats-the-wise-api
- https://docs.wise.com/

Implication for Twynex:

Twynex should not sound like a generic Wise clone. The sharper wedge is not "send money internationally"; it is enabling US payment relationships to work inside local international payment experiences such as QR, wallet, NFC, and merchant acceptance.

### Nium

Positioning:

- Real-time global payments infrastructure.
- Collect, convert, and send funds to accounts, cards, and wallets in 190+ countries.
- B2B infrastructure for banks, fintechs, payroll, marketplaces, AI, and travel.

Useful references:

- https://www.nium.com/
- https://www.nium.com/products

Implication for Twynex:

Nium owns broad money movement language. Twynex should present a more focused corridor/orchestration story around American payment rails and international local acceptance.

### Thunes

Positioning:

- Borderless payment network.
- Direct connections to local payment brands, wallets, banks, PSPs, and mobile money.
- Single network to reduce fragmented local integrations.

Useful references:

- https://www.thunes.com/about-us/
- https://www.thunes.com/insights/solutions/breaking-down-barriers-inside-thunes-borderless-payment-network/

Implication for Twynex:

Thunes validates the market language around fragmentation, direct local connections, and interoperability. Twynex can use similar problem framing but should anchor it in US-side bank/issuer relationships and traveler/business payment use cases.

### TerraPay

Positioning:

- Regulated cross-border network for wallets, bank accounts, cards, and merchant acceptance.
- "One network" style language: global reach, real-time delivery, compliance, reconciliation, and reporting.

Useful reference:

- https://www.terrapay.com/

Implication for Twynex:

TerraPay reinforces that trust, control, compliance, and reporting are important website pillars. Twynex should make its regulated-partner model very visible.

### dLocal

Positioning:

- Emerging-market payment infrastructure.
- Local payment methods, pay-ins, payouts, dynamic FX, and cross-border settlement through one platform.
- Strong focus on alternative payment methods and emerging-market complexity.

Useful references:

- https://www.dlocal.com/
- https://www.dlocal.com/our-solution/payins/

Implication for Twynex:

dLocal frames local payment method access as a growth unlock. Twynex should borrow that strategic logic, but apply it to US banks, issuers, travelers, and local acceptance abroad.

### Rapyd

Positioning:

- Fintech-as-a-service for collections, payouts, wallets, issuing, and embedded financial services.
- One platform for global payment methods and disbursement.

Useful references:

- https://www.rapyd.net/company/about-rapyd/
- https://docs.rapyd.net/en/rapyd-disburse.html

Implication for Twynex:

Rapyd's breadth means Twynex needs focus. The website should avoid trying to present every fintech infrastructure product at once.

### Airwallex

Positioning:

- Global financial platform for payments, treasury, spend, and embedded finance.
- Strong B2B finance operations story.

Useful reference:

- https://www.airwallex.com/global

Implication for Twynex:

Airwallex is broader business finance infrastructure. Twynex should stay closer to cross-border payment orchestration and local rail access.

### Visa Direct

Positioning:

- Global money movement network for domestic and cross-border payments.
- Fast, secure, card/network-enabled real-time eligible transactions.

Useful references:

- https://corporate.visa.com/en/products/visa-direct/send.html
- https://www.visa.com/en-us/products/visa-direct

Implication for Twynex:

Card networks already own global reach and trust. Twynex should present itself as complementary where local QR, wallet, bank, and NFC rails matter.

## Competitive Gaps Twynex Can Own

Twynex can position around the gap between:

- US payment rails that are modernizing domestically.
- Local international rails that are powerful but fragmented.
- Travelers and businesses that need local acceptance at the point of payment.
- Banks and issuers that want cross-border revenue without building every local corridor themselves.

Possible positioning line:

> Twynex is the orchestration layer that helps American payment relationships work inside local payment experiences abroad.

## Website Messaging Lessons

- Lead with the problem before naming the platform.
- Use plain claims: faster, local, compliant, partner-led, auditable.
- Explain "one integration" carefully. If Twynex does not yet provide one universal API, frame it as a partner-led orchestration model rather than finished global coverage.
- Do not compete head-on with broad global payout networks. Show a specific wedge and roadmap.
- Make compliance a core product attribute, not an afterthought.
- Present local acceptance as the practical user benefit and partner revenue as the business benefit.
