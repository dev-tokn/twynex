## Core Thesis

Twynex aims to globalize American payment rails by connecting US-side payment relationships into international local payment systems.

The deck frames the opportunity around a mismatch:

- US domestic payment infrastructure is modernizing through Zelle, FedNow, stablecoins, and deposit-token style flows.
- International on-ramping and off-ramping still depends heavily on legacy FX, settlement, and correspondent banking infrastructure.
- Local payment systems abroad are becoming dominant, but US customers often cannot use them directly.

Twynex's proposed role is cross-border transaction orchestration, with Wise-like disparate settlement and netting handled through partner banks.

## Initial Use Cases

### Cross-Border QR Payments

American tourists pay merchants in other countries using local QR systems, such as UPI in India or PromptPay in Thailand.

Deck details:

- Launch target: Q4 2026.
- Initial rollout: 3 countries in Southeast Asia.
- International partner role: destination-country banks providing local merchant distribution and payouts.
- Identified partner markets: Sri Lanka, Egypt, Malaysia, and Vietnam.
- Revenue streams: KYC charges, transaction charges, and revenue share.
- 2030 projected ARR from deck: USD 24.48M.

### Local Cards For Business Travelers

USA cards issued to international travelers, creating additional revenue for US issuers.

Deck details:

- Launch target: Q1 2027.
- Initial markets: India and Malaysia.
- Target customers: exporters and business travelers.
- Partner role: international resellers providing market access and collection services.
- Revenue streams: transaction charges, platform fees, and revenue share.
- 2030 projected ARR from deck: USD 23.15M.

### Future Use Cases

- Immigrants paying bills for relatives in their countries of origin using their US account.
- American tourists paying merchants through local wallets such as Bizum in Spain.
- Local acquiring for merchants to reduce additional costs and risks from international transactions.
- American tourists using a common virtual NFC card for metro and transit payments in cities such as Dubai.

## Compliance And Risk Principles

The deck emphasizes a regulated-partner operating model:

- Twynex partners only with banks and other regulated entities.
- Payment flows run through accounts owned and operated by partner banks.
- Twynex does not assume fiduciary risk or responsibility.
- KYC is performed by partner banks in the USA.
- Sensitive data should remain in regulated partner systems where possible.
- Data at rest and in transit should use industry-standard encryption.
- Data interchange should use ISO 20022 where appropriate.
- Card and NFC messaging should align with Mastercard, Visa, and PCI-DSS requirements.
- Audit trails and proof of provenance should support AMLA 2020, OFAC, SARs, and CFT controls.
